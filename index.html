U.S Health and Retirement Study


The University of Michigan Health and Retirement Study (HRS) is a longitudinal panel study that surveys a representative sample of approximately 20,000 people in America, supported by the National Institute on Aging (NIA U01AG009740) and the Social Security Administration.


Dissemination


I manage the HRS bibliography by keeping 2500+ citations up to date to be annually reviewed by the National Institutes of Health (NIH). Responsibilites include creating and deleting citations, organizing and recording specific studies and tracking publications by the HRS staff.




Accessing CMS Data Graphic


Research files created by the NIA Data LINKAGE Program (LINKAGE) link HRS data to detailed information about health care utilization from records maintained by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS).

Multiple rounds of application and approval are circulated between organizations for access to CMS data. I was in charge of creating a flowchart graphic to capture this process to be posted on the HRS website.

Tools & Technologies: Adobe Illustrator




Getting Started HRS Guidebook


HRS is creating a new guidebook to help facilitate user participation within the study. My responsibilities in this project include re-designing and converting 20+ figures to be used in the official print version.

Tools & Technologies: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, HTML, CSS




Cover Pages


User Guides and Documentation Reports are designed to provide analysts with documentation about the concepts, measures and questions in the HRS surveys. These reports expand upon the information found in codebooks, questionnaires and data descriptions.

I was in charge of standardizing the cover pages on each of these guidebooks to provide consistent information reflected by HRS. This is an ongoing project being actively updated on the HRS website

Tools & Technologies: Adobe Acrobat
